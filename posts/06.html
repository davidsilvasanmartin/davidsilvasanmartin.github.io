Negative Binomial

Suppose our random experiment is to perform a sequence of Bernoulli trials \( X = (X_1, X_2, \ldots)\), each with success parameter \( p \in (0, 1]\). The number of successes in the first \(n\) trials:

\begin{align} Y_n = \sum_{i=1}^n X_i \end{align}

has the binomial distribution with parameters \(n\) and \(p\).

Then, the random variable \(V_k\) that gives the trial number of the \(k\)th success is known as the negative binomial distribution, \(NB(k, p)\):

\begin{align} V_k = \min{\{n \in \mathbb{N}_{+} \mid Y_n = k\}} \end{align}

It has 2 parameters: the stopping parameter k and the success probability p. Its pmf is

\begin{align} P\{V_k = n\} = {n-1 \choose k-1} p^k (1-p)^{n-k}, \quad n \in \{k, k+1, k+2, \ldots\} \end{align}
And:
  • \( E[V_k] = \frac{k}{p} \), that is: on average, we need to perform \(\frac{k}{p}\) Bernoulli trials until we obtain the \(k\)th success.
  • \( Var(V_k) = \frac{k(1-p)}{p^2} \)
