A coin, having probability p of coming up heads, is to be successively flipped until the first head appears. What is the expected number of flips required?

First of all, let us define a probability space \( (\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)\) for this problem.

  • \( \Omega = \left\{ (w_1, w_2, w_3, \ldots) \mid w_i \in \{0, 1\}, \, i = 1, 2, \ldots \right\}\). Each of the elements of \(\Omega\) is an infinite sequence of 0's and 1's. The set \(\Omega\) is then uncountable (see this SO post).
  • \(\mathcal{F} = \mathcal{P}(\Omega)\), the superset of \(\Omega\).
  • The probability is defined as follows. Let \(A_k \subseteq \{0, 1\}^k\) be a set of length \(k\) of \(0\)s and \(1\)s. Then, we define: $$ P\left(\{( w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_{k-1}, w_k, w_{k+1}, \ldots) \mid (w_1, w_2, \ldots, w_{k-1}, w_k) \in A_k \}\right) = \frac{|A_k|}{2^k} $$

It can be proven that the probabilities for all elements of \(\mathcal{F}\) are well defined, and thus that \( (\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)\) is a probability space.
